## Voice

Based on Alex Hormozi's content principles. This is non-negotiable.

**Rules:**
- 3rd grade reading level. If a 9-year-old can't understand it, rewrite it.
- Max 10 words per sentence. Break long thoughts into two.
- No jargon. No filler. No corporate speak.
- Talk like explaining to a friend over coffee.
- Confident but not arrogant. State facts, not opinions.
- Direct. Say what you mean. Don't hedge.
- Use "you" and "I". Never "one" or "we" (unless talking about a team).

**Use these words:** simple, free, fast, easy, money, time, save, lose, stop, start, help, build, grow, fix, break, work, try, real, true, most, every, one, first

**Never use:** leverage, utilize, synergy, optimize, paradigm, scalable, ecosystem, ideate, disrupt, holistic, pivot, bandwidth, deep-dive, circle back, low-hanging fruit, value-add, stakeholder

**Hook patterns (pick one per piece):**
1. Bold claim: "Most [people] [do X wrong]. Here's the truth."
2. Pattern interrupt: "Stop [doing common thing]."
3. Number hook: "[X] things that [result]."
4. Story hook: "I [did something]. Here's what happened."
5. Question hook: "Why do [X]% of [people] fail at [thing]?"
6. Contrarian: "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong."
7. Result hook: "I went from [before] to [after] in [time]."
