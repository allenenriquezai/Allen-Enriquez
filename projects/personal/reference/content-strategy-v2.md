# Content Strategy V2 — AI for Business People

> V1 (AI sales systems only) stays in CONTEXT.md as reference. V2 widens the lane for volume + reach.

## The Lane

"AI for business and work people — taught by a sales guy who actually uses it."

Allen is NOT an AI expert. He's a sales guy who built AI systems for his real job and shares everything. The audience learns AI through his real results + broad tutorials.

## Content Split

| Lane | % | What | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Broad** | 70% | Tutorials, use cases, fundamentals | Tools/use cases Allen has tested or learned. Doesn't need to be his daily system. |
| **Proof** | 30% | Allen's real systems + results | EPS automation, real numbers, real workflows. Must be real. Anonymize company. |

**Why 70/30:** Zero audience right now. Need reach first, trust second. Broad content = more topics = easier to batch = algorithm gets more data. Proof content = credibility + differentiation. Flip to 50/50 once audience is established.

## Content Filter

Every piece must pass ALL five:

1. **"I didn't know AI could do that"** OR **"I need to try this"** — if neither, it's too basic or too vague
2. **Beginner-friendly** — a total beginner can understand it in 60 seconds
3. **Verified** — Allen tried it OR it's proven to work
4. **CCN fit** — hits Core (buyers) + Casual (followers) + New (strangers)
5. **Viewer-first angle** — their pain → your fix. Not your flex → their admiration.

## CCN Fit (Sam Gaudet)

Every piece must work for three audiences at once:

- **Core:** Service business owners, professionals who'd pay for AI setup
- **Casual:** Filipino VAs, professionals following to learn, people not ready to buy
- **New:** Total strangers who've never seen Allen before

**Test:** "Would a VA in Manila, a tradie in Brisbane, AND someone who's never heard of me all get value from this?"

If it only hits one segment → rethink the angle, not the topic.

## Script Frameworks

### H.E.I.T. (Reels — 60-120 sec)

Primary framework for all short-form. Used in 90% of Gaudet/Martell content.

| Beat | What | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Context + contrarian/surprising claim + open a loop | 0-3 sec |
| **Explain** | Clarify the concept. Keep it dead simple. | 3-15 sec |
| **Illustrate** | Story, analogy, or example. Tie new info to something they already know. | 15-45 sec |
| **Takeaway** | One clear action they can do RIGHT NOW. | 45-60 sec |

**Hook rules:**
- Must land in first 3 seconds
- Every word carries weight — no filler
- Open a loop they need to close
- Start with THEIR problem, not YOUR system

### Hormozi (YouTube — 7-12 min)

For long-form only. Full retention framework.

| Phase | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 0-5s | Pattern interrupt — bold claim or surprising number |
| **Proof** | 5-20s | Your credibility — specific results, not titles |
| **Promise** | 20-40s | What they'll walk away with — be specific |
| **Plan** | 40-60s | How the video is structured — "3 parts" or "5 steps" |
| **Content** | 1-8min | Deliver value in chapters. Open loops between sections. |
| **Reward** | 8-9min | Big takeaway — one sentence they remember |
| **CTA** | 9-10min | Subscribe + "Watch this next" |

## Angle Rules

The #1 lesson from Allen's first content: "4 Levels of AI" worked (educational, simple, viewer can self-assess). "My AI starts work before me" flopped (flex, no viewer payoff).

**Rule: Start with THEIR pain, not YOUR system.**

| Wrong angle (flex) | Right angle (viewer-first) |
|---|---|
| "My AI starts work before I do" | "Your morning routine wastes 2 hours. Here's the free fix." |
| "I built a quote system" | "Stop spending 45 minutes on quotes. Do this instead." |
| "4 Levels of AI in Business" | "Which level are you? Most people are stuck at Level 1." |
| "I automated my CRM" | "You're losing clients because you forget to follow up." |

**Pattern:** Outcome hook → show the magic → reveal it's AI → one thing they can try now.

## Content Types

### Quick Tutorials (Sabrina Ramonov style) — 40% of broad lane
- Screen recording + simple narration
- "Here's how to do X with AI in 2 minutes"
- Show the tool, show the result
- Assume viewer has never touched AI
- One task per video. Never two.

### Use Case Showcases (Dan Martell style) — 30% of broad lane
- "X things AI can do for Y"
- List format with quick demo of each
- Emphasize speed + no code needed
- Infinitely repeatable template

### Fundamentals (wrapped in outcomes) — 30% of broad lane
- "What is [AI concept]?" — but framed as outcome
- "AI agents explained in 60 seconds"
- Simple analogies. "It's like hiring a worker who never sleeps."
- These build audience literacy so deeper content lands later

### Proof/Results (Allen's systems) — 100% of proof lane
- Real numbers, real systems, real before/after
- Always anonymize company/clients
- Show the dashboard, the workflow, the result
- These convert followers into clients

## Production Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Format mix** | Talking head + screen recordings + animations |
| **Language** | 3rd grade reading level. Max 10 words per sentence. No jargon. |
| **Volume** | 2 reels/day minimum |
| **Distribution** | Blotato for cross-posting + scheduling (all platforms from one post) |
| **70/20/10** | 70% replicate proven formats, 20% variations, 10% experimental |
| **Topic > production** | A good topic with basic editing beats a boring topic with fancy editing |
| **R.I.A.** | Results in Advance — give enough free value that they get a result before paying |

## Voice (unchanged from V1)

All V1 voice rules still apply. See CONTEXT.md. Non-negotiable:
- 3rd grade reading level
- Max 10 words per sentence
- No jargon, no filler, no corporate speak
- Use: simple, free, fast, easy, money, time, save, lose, stop, start
- Never use: leverage, utilize, synergy, optimize, paradigm, scalable

## When to Flip the Ratio

Move from 70/30 (broad/proof) toward 50/50 when:
- 1,000+ followers on primary platform
- Consistent 2+ reels/day for 30 days
- DMs asking "how did you do that?" regularly
- First client inquiry from content
