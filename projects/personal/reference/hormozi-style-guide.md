# Hormozi Content Style Guide

Based on Alex Hormozi's content principles. Allen's agents reference this for voice, structure, and format.

---

## Voice Rules

- 3rd grade reading level. If a 9-year-old can't understand it, rewrite it.
- Max 10 words per sentence. Break long thoughts into two sentences.
- No jargon. No filler. No corporate speak. No fancy words.
- Talk like you're explaining to a friend over coffee.
- Confident but not arrogant. State facts, not opinions.
- Direct. Say what you mean. Don't hedge.
- Use "you" and "I". Never "one" or "we" (unless talking about a team).

## Word Banks

**Use these words:** simple, free, fast, easy, money, time, save, lose, stop, start, help, build, grow, fix, break, work, try, real, true, most, every, one, first

**Never use:** leverage, utilize, synergy, optimize, paradigm, scalable, ecosystem, ideate, disrupt, holistic, pivot, bandwidth, deep-dive, circle back, low-hanging fruit, value-add, stakeholder

## Hook Patterns (pick one per piece)

1. **Bold claim:** "Most [people] [do X wrong]. Here's the truth."
2. **Pattern interrupt:** "Stop [doing common thing]."
3. **Number hook:** "[X] things that [result]."
4. **Story hook:** "I [did something]. Here's what happened."
5. **Question hook:** "Why do [X]% of [people] fail at [thing]?"
6. **Contrarian:** "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong."
7. **Result hook:** "I went from [before] to [after] in [time]."

The hook must land in the first 3 seconds (video) or first line (text).

---

## Script Structures

### Reel (30-60 seconds)
```
Hook (0-3 sec): [Pattern interrupt or bold claim — one sentence]
Problem (3-15 sec): [Why this matters — relatable pain, simple words]
Solution (15-45 sec): [What to do — 2-3 steps max, show don't tell]
CTA (45-60 sec): [Follow for more / Comment X / Save this]
```
- One idea only. Never two.
- Under 150 words total.
- Fast pacing. No pauses. No "umm".
- Text overlays for key words/numbers.

### YouTube (5-10 minutes)
```
Hook (0-30 sec): [Bold promise or result — why they should keep watching]
Stakes (30-90 sec): [What they lose by not knowing this]
Framework (90-360 sec): [3-5 steps. Each step = one simple idea. Examples for each.]
Proof (360-480 sec): [Real results. Screen shares. Numbers.]
CTA (480-600 sec): [Subscribe + "Watch this next" with specific video mention]
```
- One idea expanded. Deep, not wide.
- Chapter markers for each section.
- Under 1500 words for the script.
- B-roll or screen share during Framework section.

### Facebook Group Post (50-100 words)
```
[Value-first. Answer a question or share a result.]
[1-2 short paragraphs. Simple language.]
[End with a question that invites replies.]
```
- No links. No selling. No pitching.
- Pure value. Help someone.
- If they ask how in comments, then you can DM.

### LinkedIn Post (under 200 words)
```
[Hook line — problem or stat]
[2-3 short paragraphs — insight + example]
[Engagement question]
```
- Same rules as before. Problem first, metric, question at end.

## Pacing Rules

| Format | Pacing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Reel | Fast. 1 idea per 10 seconds. | Cut between points. No long pauses. |
| YouTube | Medium. 1 idea per 60-90 seconds. | Breathe between sections. Let ideas land. |
| FB post | N/A (text) | Short paragraphs. White space between lines. |
| LinkedIn | N/A (text) | Same as FB — scannable. |

## Visual/Filming Notes (for Allen)

- **Face to camera.** Tight frame. Head and shoulders.
- **Look at the lens**, not the screen.
- **Hand gestures** for emphasis — point, count on fingers, open palms.
- **Text overlays** for key numbers and takeaways (add in editing).
- **Quick cuts** between points. Remove dead air.
- **Good lighting.** Natural light or ring light. No dark rooms.
- **Clean background.** Simple. Not distracting.
- **Energy.** Talk like you care. Not hype, but conviction.

## Content Pillars (Allen's brand)

1. **AI automation results** — proof that this works (metrics, before/after)
2. **How AI works (simple)** — explain Claude Code, prompts, agents to beginners
3. **Business owner pain points** — relatable problems (slow quotes, missed leads, admin overload)
4. **Behind the scenes** — show the build process (screen recordings, live coding)
5. **Contrarian takes** — challenge common beliefs (you don't need more leads, you need better follow-up)

Rotate pillars across the week. Never post the same pillar two days in a row.

## Audience Notes (Filipino market — initial test)

- Many are VAs or aspiring VAs who want to earn in USD.
- Value hard work, resourcefulness, family.
- Respond well to practical, step-by-step content.
- Use Filipino-accessible examples (not US-centric references).
- Price sensitivity is high — emphasize "free" and "DIY" before paid.
- English is a second language for most — keep it simple. Really simple.
- Facebook is the primary platform in the Philippines. Instagram and TikTok are secondary.

## Quality Checklist (run before every piece)

- [ ] 3rd grade reading level?
- [ ] Hook grabs attention in first line / 3 seconds?
- [ ] One idea only?
- [ ] Includes a specific number or result?
- [ ] Ends with CTA or engagement question?
- [ ] Under word limit for the format?
- [ ] No jargon or banned words?
- [ ] No client names or identifying details?
- [ ] Culturally appropriate for target audience?
- [ ] Would Allen actually say this out loud?
