## Delivery Model (Industry Standard)

| Aspect | How it works |
|---|---|
| API accounts | Client owns their own keys (OpenAI, Anthropic). Provider sets up, client pays usage. |
| Infrastructure | Provider hosts n8n on their server. One VPS runs multiple client instances. Client gets a portal. |
| Pricing structure | One-time setup fee + monthly maintenance retainer (hybrid model dominates) |
| Tools | n8n (most common for agencies), Make (mid-tier), Zapier (simple/expensive) |
| Handoff vs managed | "Managed" = recurring revenue. "Build and handoff" = one-time but easier with limited time. |

## Key Risks

1. Allen has 1-2 hours/day for client work — service must be templated, not custom
2. Delivery model (whose Claude account?) still unsolved
3. PH clients may not pay enough to sustain — need AU/US/UK clients for real revenue
4. Free builds may not convert to testimonials if clients don't engage
5. Market is moving fast — what's valuable today may be commoditised in 6 months

## Validated Decisions (April 2026)

1. VAs are AUDIENCE, not customers. Serve them with free content.
2. Service businesses are the primary CUSTOMER segment.
3. Content creators with budget are secondary CUSTOMER segment.
4. PH market first for credibility, AU/US/UK for revenue.
5. Free trials for first 3-5 clients (people Allen knows), then charge.
6. Lead with the person, not the service — build what they need.
7. Content IS the funnel. No cold outreach, no ads (yet).
