# Outreach System -- SOP

Content brings people in. Outreach starts conversations. Launch both together -- content goes live, outreach starts the same day.

## Target Segments (Ranked by ICP Score)

### Segment 1 -- Filipino Virtual Assistants (34/40)

**Why them:** 1.5M Filipino VAs. AI is replacing basic VAs right now -- this is a survival issue. VAs who upskill get 2-3x higher rates. VAs who don't get replaced. The fear is real and the pain is immediate.

**Need:** 9/10 -- 60-70% of VA work (data entry, email, scheduling, chat support) is automatable today.
**Budget:** 6/10 -- Entry-level VAs are tight (PHP 25-40k/month). Mid-to-specialist VAs (PHP 45-150k+) can pay PHP 5-20k/month. Many already pay for Canva, ChatGPT, Grammarly.
**Urgency:** 9/10 -- Clients are replacing basic VAs with ChatGPT. AI-skilled VAs command 2-3x rates. Existential.
**Reachability:** 10/10 -- Massive FB groups (50k-200k+ members). 22M LinkedIn users in PH. Public Upwork/OnlineJobs.ph profiles.

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups: "Virtual Assistants Philippines", "Filipino VA Community", VA upskilling groups
- OnlineJobs.ph profiles
- LinkedIn (search "virtual assistant" + Philippines)
- People posting questions about AI, productivity, upskilling

**Hook:** "AI is replacing basic VAs. Here's how to become the VA clients can't replace."

**What to automate for them:** Email drafting, social media scheduling, data entry, client reporting, calendar management, chat support, lead research, content repurposing.

**Approach:** Post free AI tutorial clips in VA groups. DM VAs who engage. Lead with free content, convert to done-for-you automation setup.

### Segment 2 -- Filipino Real Estate Agents/Brokers (31/40)

**Why them:** Agents who respond faster to inquiries win deals. Most do everything manually via Viber/Messenger. Time saved = more deals closed. PH real estate market is USD 94.4B and growing.

**Need:** 8/10 -- Hours wasted on lead follow-up, listing management, viewing scheduling. All manual.
**Budget:** 7/10 -- Average broker earns PHP 61k/month. Commission on a PHP 5M property = PHP 150-300k. Can easily justify PHP 5-20k/month.
**Urgency:** 7/10 -- Competition is fierce. Digital marketing is now essential. Stragglers are losing deals.
**Reachability:** 9/10 -- Extremely active on Facebook with large real estate groups. Use FB Pages for listings. Public profiles.

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups: "The Real Estate Group Philippines", PH property listing groups
- Facebook Pages (agents posting listings)
- Instagram (property photos)
- People posting manual listings or asking about lead generation

**Hook:** "You lost 3 buyers today because you couldn't reply fast enough. Fix that in 10 minutes."

**What to automate for them:** Lead inquiry auto-response, listing distribution, viewing schedule management, client follow-up sequences, social media property posts.

**Approach:** Join PH real estate FB groups. Show automated lead follow-up demos. DM agents posting listings manually.

### Segment 3 -- Recruitment/Staffing Agencies (31/40)

**Why them:** PH staffing sector is $42B, growing 14-16% annually. 800+ agencies competing for the same talent. Agency that screens and presents candidates first wins the placement. AI screening gives massive competitive advantage.

**Need:** 9/10 -- Sourcing, screening resumes, scheduling interviews, status updates -- all intensely repetitive.
**Budget:** 8/10 -- One PHP 600k/year placement = PHP 90-150k fee. Already invest in ATS, job boards, LinkedIn Recruiter. PHP 5-20k/month is trivial.
**Urgency:** 8/10 -- Speed wins placements. Market growing fast. Competitors are adopting AI.
**Reachability:** 6/10 -- LinkedIn mostly. B2B sale -- needs demos/meetings, not quick DM conversions. Longer sales cycle.

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn (search PH recruitment agency owners, HR managers)
- Agency websites (cold email)
- Some recruitment industry FB groups

**Hook:** "Your competitor screened 200 resumes in 15 minutes this morning. You're still doing it manually."

**What to automate for them:** Resume screening/ranking, candidate-job matching, interview scheduling, status update emails, job posting distribution, candidate sourcing.

**Approach:** LinkedIn outreach to agency owners. Content showing AI screening demos. Offer free workflow audits. Higher ticket, longer cycle, very sticky.

## Prospect Tiers

### Tier 1 -- High-Value Targets (Personalized DM + Phone)

People with audiences, buying power, or influence. One partnership can reach thousands.

**Examples:** Top VA influencers, real estate team leaders, recruitment agency owners, large FB group admins.

**How to find them:**
- Search Facebook/IG for people with large followings in target segments
- Check who runs the biggest groups
- Look at who gets the most engagement in communities
- LinkedIn search for agency owners and managers

**Approach:**
- Research them first. Watch their content. Know what they do.
- Phone call if you can get a number (warmest approach)
- If no number, send a fully personalized DM referencing their specific content
- Never batch these. One at a time. Fully custom.

### Tier 2 -- Group Members (Semi-Automated DMs)

Active members of Facebook groups in target segments. Lower individual value but high volume.

**Examples:** VAs posting questions, agents posting listings, sellers asking about tools.

**How to find them:**
- People who post questions about productivity, AI, tech, being overwhelmed
- Active commenters (not lurkers -- they won't reply)
- People engaging with your content in groups

**Approach:**
- Use segment-specific templates (below) but swap in their name + what they posted about
- Semi-automated -- same structure, different details
- Volume play -- more messages = more conversations

### Tier 3 -- Allen's Network (LinkedIn + Facebook)

Friends, former colleagues, people Allen knows personally.

**Approach:**
- Fully personalized. No templates. These are real relationships.
- Start with people who would benefit most from AI automation
- After the first message, ask for introductions to their connections

## Facebook DMs (Primary Channel)

### Safe Limits -- Don't Get Flagged

| Action | Daily Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| New message threads (non-friends) | 10-15 | Stay under 15. Facebook restricts at ~20-25 |
| New message threads (friends) | 30-40 | Friends are safer. Higher limit. |
| Friend requests | 10-15 | Space them out. Don't send 15 at once. |
| Group joins | 3-5 | Join too many fast = flagged |

**Safety rules:**
- Spread messages across the day. Not all at once.
- If Facebook warns you or blocks messaging, stop for 48 hours.
- Older accounts (like Allen's) have higher limits than new ones.
- Don't copy-paste the exact same text to multiple people. Change words.
- Mix in normal activity (liking, commenting, posting) between DMs.

### Templates -- Facebook (by Segment)

**VAs -- Tier 1 (Influencer/Group Admin):**

>

>
> Can I send you a quick video showing how it works?

**VAs -- Tier 2 (Group Member):**
> Hey [name]! Saw your post in [group] about [their problem].
>
> I had the same issue. I set up a simple AI tool that handles it automatically now. Took about 10 minutes.
>
> Want me to show you how?

**Real Estate -- Tier 1 (Top Agent/Team Leader):**
> Hey [name], saw your listings in [group]. You're clearly doing volume.
>
> I built an AI tool that auto-responds to property inquiries and follows up with buyers automatically. Agents I know are closing more deals because they never miss a lead.
>

**Real Estate -- Tier 2 (Agent posting listings):**

**Friend/Connection (Tier 3):**

> Thought of you because [specific reason]. Want to see how it works?

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Touch | When | Message |
|---|---|---|
| Touch 1 | Day 1 | Opening message (templates above) |
| Touch 2 | 48h later, no reply | Share a tip or short video. No pitch. |
| Touch 3 | 5 days later, no reply | "No worries if you're busy. Offer stands if you ever want help." |
| After Touch 3 | No reply | Stop. Mark as cold in CRM. |

## Phone Calls (Daily Support)

Phone calls are a daily habit. Use for warm leads, referrals, and anyone you have a number for.

### When to Call
- After researching the prospect (know what they do, what they need)
- Best times: 10 AM - 12 PM and 2 PM - 4 PM Philippine time
- Avoid Mondays and Fridays

### Call Structure
1. Introduce yourself. Keep it short (10 seconds).
2. Reference something specific about them ("I saw your post about...")
3. Ask one question about their biggest time-waster
4. Listen. Let them talk.
5. If relevant, offer to show them your system
6. If not a fit, ask who else might benefit

### After the Call
- Log in CRM: name, date, outcome, next step
- If warm: send a follow-up DM or email within 1 hour
- If cold: mark as cold, move on

## Rules

1. **Take action.** Every day, outreach happens. No excuses.
2. **Facebook and Instagram DMs are primary.** Phone calls are daily support.
3. **Never pitch in the first message.** Help first. Pitch later.
4. **Personalize everything for Tier 1.** Templates are for Tier 2 only.
5. **Respect the limits.** Getting flagged kills the channel for weeks.
6. **If they say no or ghost after Touch 3, stop.** Move on.
7. **Track everything.** If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.
8. **Filipino prospects:** Simple English. Pesos not dollars. "Sideline" and "extra income" resonate. Be humble.
9. **Zero cost.** No paid tools, no InMail credits, no ads. All organic.
10. **Volume matters.** More conversations = more clients. But never sacrifice quality for Tier 1.
11. **Ask for referrals.** Every warm conversation ends with "Who else might find this useful?"
12. **Content and outreach launch together.** Content is the proof. Outreach is the push.
