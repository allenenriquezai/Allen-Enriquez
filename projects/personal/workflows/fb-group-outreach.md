# Facebook Group Outreach — SOP

The content manager reads this when generating Facebook group content.

---

## Target Groups

### Primary (Filipino VAs)
- Filipino Virtual Assistants (VA) groups
- OFW (Overseas Filipino Workers) side-hustle groups
- Filipino freelancer communities
- "Work from home Philippines" groups

### Secondary
- AI automation / no-code groups
- Small business owner groups (general)
- Allen's personal network (friends, colleagues)

---

## Posting Cadence

- 2-3 value posts per week across all groups
- Max 1 post per group per week (don't get flagged)
- Rotate groups so each group sees a fresh post every 1-2 weeks
- Best days: Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday
- Best time: 8-10 AM Philippine time (same as AEST)

---

## Post Types

### Type 1: Quick Win
Share one specific thing they can do today.
```
I just found out you can [do X] with [free tool].

It takes 5 minutes and saves [Y hours] per week.

Has anyone here tried this?
```

### Type 2: Result Share
Show a real result (anonymized).
```
I helped a business owner automate their [process].

Before: [pain]. After: [result].

What's the one thing you'd automate in your work if you could?
```

### Type 3: Question
Ask something that starts a conversation.
```
Quick question for VAs here:

What's the most repetitive task you do every day?

I'm building a list of the top 10 tasks AI can handle for VAs.
```

### Type 4: Education
Teach one simple concept.
```
A lot of people ask me what AI agents are.

Simple version: it's like hiring a virtual assistant that works 24/7 and never forgets.

You tell it what to do once. It does it every time.

Would a free guide on setting one up be helpful?
```

---

## Rules

1. **Never pitch in groups.** No links. No "check out my service". No selling.
2. **Value first, always.** Every post must help someone even if they never buy.
3. **End with a question.** Gets replies. Replies boost visibility.
4. **If someone asks "how?" in comments** — reply briefly, then DM with more detail.
5. **DM sequence after engagement:**
   - DM 1: "Hey [name], saw your comment about [topic]. Happy to share more — what's your situation?"
   - DM 2 (if they reply): Share a specific tip or resource. No pitch.
   - DM 3 (if warm): "I actually set this up for businesses for free — want me to show you how it works?"
6. **Read group rules** before posting. Some groups ban certain topics. Follow their rules.
7. **Be a member first.** Comment on other people's posts before posting your own. Build presence.
8. **Track in content tracker** which groups you've posted in and when.

---

## Filipino-Specific Notes

- Use simple English. Many group members speak English as a second language.
- Peso references work better than USD for relatable examples.
- "Sideline" and "extra income" resonate more than "business" or "startup".
- Family-oriented framing works well ("more time with family", "work from home").
- Be respectful. Filipino online communities value politeness and humility.
- Don't position as guru. Position as fellow worker sharing what's working.
