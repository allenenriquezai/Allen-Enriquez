# PH Outbound — ICP

3 segments. VAs and recruitment are primary. Realtors are secondary.

---

## 1. Filipino VAs (primary)

### Who
Solo Filipino virtual assistants. Work for foreign clients. Some run small VA teams.

### Pain
- Scared AI will take their job.
- Want to charge more but don't know how.
- Doing the same tasks over and over. Burnt out.
- Clients ask for "AI skills" they don't have.

### Budget
$47 to $300. They have some money. Not a lot.

### Where Allen finds them
- FB groups (PH VA communities)
- LinkedIn (search "virtual assistant Philippines")
- Kalibrr and JobStreet candidate listings
- IG hashtags (#filipinoVA, #virtualassistantph)

### Disqualifiers
- Brand new VAs with no clients. Can't pay.
- Already running an agency with full systems. Don't need help.
- Too suspicious. Won't reply to anything that smells like sales.

---

## 2. PH Recruitment + VA agencies (primary)

### Who
Small to mid PH recruitment firms. Or VA agencies placing offshore staff. 5 to 50 staff.

### Pain
- Manual candidate sourcing. Hours per role.
- Slow follow-up with clients. Lose deals.
- CRM is a spreadsheet. Or nothing.
- Owner does sales AND ops.

### Budget
$300 to $1,500 for one-off automation builds.

### Where Allen finds them
- Google Places API (search "recruitment agency Manila/Cebu/Davao")
- BusinessList.ph
- JobStreet employer pages
- LinkedIn company search

### Disqualifiers
- Big firm with internal tech team.
- 1-person shop. No volume to automate.
- No website, no real online presence.

---

## 3. PH Real estate brokerages (secondary)

### Who
Real estate brokerages in Metro Manila, Cebu, Davao. 3 to 30 agents.

### Pain
- Leads come in via FB and IG DMs. Get lost.
- Agents don't follow up.
- No CRM. Or one nobody uses.
- Owner spends weekends chasing agents for updates.

### Budget
$300 to $1,500. Some pay more for full setup.

### Where Allen finds them
- Google Places API (search "real estate brokerage [PH city]")
- FB pages
- LinkedIn

### Disqualifiers
- Solo agents. No team. No need for a system.
- Big developer-owned brokerages. They have in-house tech.
- No active social presence. Means no lead flow to fix.
